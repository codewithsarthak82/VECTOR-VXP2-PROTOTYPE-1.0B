Orion Spacetech: VECTOR VXP2 Development Synthesis
Founder: Sarthak | Project: Predictive Engine Health Monitoring

1. The Core Innovation (Proof of Concept)
We started by proving our core mathematical hypothesis against the NASA CMAPSS dataset. By analyzing over 20,000 engine cycles, we engineered a robust data pipeline that maps complex, irregular sensor telemetry directly to an engine's degradation trajectory. Leveraging a heavily optimized Random Forest Regressor, we successfully isolated critical indicators—like High-Pressure Turbine Exit Temperature—to output highly precise Remaining Useful Life (RUL) predictions. We didn't just find correlation; we built a deterministic model that predicts engine failure within an extremely tight margin of error.

2. Velocity & Technical Execution (Platform Phase)
Proving the math was step one; shipping it at scale is step two. We rapidly transitioned our backend POC into a high-performance 'Digital Twin' SaaS platform. Moving with relentless execution velocity, we scaffolded a full-stack dashboard utilizing Next.js, Tailwind CSS, GSAP, and Framer Motion. We designed a proprietary "Aero-Dark" visual architecture optimized for complex data density, ensuring that critical edge-to-cloud telemetry—from raw pressure metrics to internal material creep—is visualized seamlessly in real-time.

3. Industry Traction
Our iteration speed and proven architecture have already translated into real-world momentum. We have secured a strategic data-sharing partnership with Trishul Aerospace to ingest vital flight data. Our mission is absolute: we are solving the aerospace industry's "Certification Bottleneck" by delivering undeniable, predictive mathematical proof of engine reliability and safety.
